## <p align = center> _Insights from RFM Analysis_

---

The dataset consists of 2,823 rows and 25 columns, providing sufficient customer transaction data for segmentation analysis.

---

The Champions segment is the most valuable customer group:

1. Highest average monetary value: 190,116.63
2. Highest purchase frequency: 5.53
3. Lowest recency: 31 days

- This indicates that these customers purchase frequently, spend the most, and interacted with the business recently.

---

The Loyal segment also demonstrates strong customer engagement:

1. Average frequency: 3.5
2. Average monetary value: 103,926.83
3. Recency around 114 days

- These customers consistently generate revenue and should be retained through loyalty strategies.

---

Customers in the At Risk segment show declining engagement:

1. Recency increased to 166 days
2. Frequency decreased compared to Champions and Loyal segments

- These customers were previously active but may stop purchasing if no retention campaigns are applied.

---

The Needs Attention segment has moderate spending behavior but declining activity:

1. Average recency: 239 days
2. Frequency: 2.53

- Personalized promotions and re-engagement campaigns could help reactivate them.

---

The Lost segment represents inactive customers:

1. Highest recency: 356 days
2. Lowest frequency: 1.95

- These customers have not interacted with the business for a long period and show low purchasing activity.

---

- Euro Shopping Channel is the most valuable customer:

1. Highest frequency: 26 purchases
2. Highest monetary value: 912,294.11

- This customer significantly contributes to total revenue and should be considered a key account.

---

- Men 'R' US Retailers has the highest recency value of 509 days, indicating a long period without purchases and a high probability of churn.

---

- Bavarian Collectables Import Co has only 1 purchase frequency, suggesting a one-time or inactive customer relationship.

---

# <p align = center> _Summary_

Overall, the RFM analysis shows a clear distinction between highly engaged high-value customers and inactive low-frequency customers, enabling targeted marketing, retention, and customer reactivation strategies.
